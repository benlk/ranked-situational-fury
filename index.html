Ranked statements of situational fury

Words to describe bad things

This is inspired by Bernard Yu's desire for "a ranked diagram of non-ablest statements of situational fury."

What is ableist?

Ableism is systematic, institutional devaluing of bodies and minds deemed deviant, abnormal, defective, subhuman, less than.
Ableism/Language, by Lydia Brown.

Lydia Brown's post about ableist language goes into depth about things to avoid, but the overarching theme is to use words that describe — rather than the situation anthropomorphically — the situation's actions or the the beholder's reaction to the situation.

Furious definitions

abhorrent
Abhorrent, a. 1. Hating, detesting, abominating, loathing. 2. Odious, offensive, shocking, repugnant, hateful, loathsome. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
Ab*hor"rent, Abhorring; detesting; having or showing abhorrence; loathing; hence, strongly opposed to; as, abhorrent thoughts. From The Gutenberg Webster's Unabridged Dictionary.
Ab*hor", To shrink back with horror, disgust, or dislike; to be contrary or averse; -- with from. "To abhor from those vices." From The Gutenberg Webster's Unabridged Dictionary.
abominable
Abominable, a. 1. Hateful, odious, detestable, horrid, horrible, execrable, nefarious, damnable, cursed, accursed, hellish. 2. Loathsome, offensive, obnoxious, foul, nauseous, nauseating, disgusting, sickening, repulsive, revolting, shocking. 3. Vile, wretched, sorry, scurvy, shabby, bad. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
A*bom"i*na*ble, Worthy of, or causing, abhorrence, as a thing of evil omen; odious in the utmost degree; very hateful; detestable; loathsome; execrable. From The Gutenberg Webster's Unabridged Dictionary.
absurd
ABSURDITY, n. A statement or belief manifestly inconsistent with one's own opinion. From The Devil's Dictionary.
Absurd, a. Unreasonable, irrational, foolish, nonsensical, ridiculous, incongruous, senseless, unwise, silly, stupid, preposterous, paradoxical, ill-judged, ill-advised, contrary to reason, contrary to the dictates of common sense. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
atrocious
Atrocious, a. Infamous, villanous, flagitious, heinous, felonious, flagrant, outrageous, enormous, grievous, diabolical, monstrous, infernal, hellish, horrible, black, very wicked. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
a circus
CIRCUS, n. A place where horses, ponies and elephants are permitted to see men, women and children acting the fool. From The Devil's Dictionary.
farcical
Farcical, a. Droll, ludicrous, ridiculous. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
Farce, n. 1. Burlesque, caricature, travesty, parody, after-piece, low comedy, ludicrous representation. 2. Mere show, ridiculous pageantry, empty parade. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
incredible
Incredible, a. Not to be credited, not to be believed, not trustworthy, not to be believed, not worthy of belief, improbable. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
ludicrous
Ludicrous, a. Laughable, comical, odd, ridiculous, droll, funny, farcical, absurd. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
preposterous
Preposterous, a. 1. Perverted, having the cart before the horse, reversed. 2. Absurd, unreasonable, ridiculous, irrational, foolish, monstrous. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
ridiculous
Ridiculous, a. Ludicrous, laughable, droll, funny, preposterous, farcical, comical, absurd. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
terrible
Terrible, a. Frightful, horrible, dreadful, formidable, redoubtable, fearful, grewsome, terrific, awful, dread, dire, tremendous. From A Dictionary of English Synonymes and Synonymous or Parallel Expressions.
unbelievable
